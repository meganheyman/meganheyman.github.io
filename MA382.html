MA 382: Introduction to Statistics

Prerequisites: MA 381 (Introduction to Probability)

Offering: M T Th F 5th hour, G308

Degree Requirements:
  • Statistics & OR Core for math majors
  • Statistics minor
  • Data Science minor

Have you had an introductory course in probability? Are you interested in hands-on data analysis? Then this is the course for you. In MA382, we dive into basic theory surrounding the field of statistics while motivating all of the topics with real data analyses. Students who take MA382 have the opportunity to

  • Dive in to basic statistical theory with the help your probability prerequisite knowledge
  • Learn the R and R Markdown statistical languages
  • Work with large, complicated datasets.
  • Complete real data analyses
  • Superior class projects can be submitted to national competitions and potentially win $$$.

    Why is statistics important?

    Individuals with strong statistical analysis skills are in high-demand. Statistician and Data Scientist positions top multiple desirable job lists online, including

    • CareerCast.com
    • U.S. News & World Reports
    • GlassDoor.com
    Essentially anyone working in STEM needs the ability to analyze data. This course gives you base knowledge to be able to perform your own data analyses.

    What kind of data analysis skills will I have after this class?

    You will be able to fit a variety of linear models to data. We cover basics of confidence intervals, hypothesis testing, study design, and sampling. Methods employed include linear regression and ANOVA. We perform both parametric (normal, t-distribution) and nonparametric (bootstrap, randomization methods) inference. Throughout the term, you will incorporate the methods through analysis of a large, messy dataset. You will also be able to interpret and create any of the graphics shown below.

    FAQ:

    How are MA382 and MA223 different?

    MA223 (Engineering Statistics) and MA382 technically cover the same statistical methods. However, the student experience in these courses is widely different. MA223 focuses on engineering applications with little formal theory presented (due to no MA381 pre-req). MA223 uses Minitab (point-and-click software) as the main analysis tool and most data analyses utilize small datasets. MA382 relies on the prior exposure to probability to dive a bit deeper into the statistical theory. For example, why do we use a sample mean to estimate the population mean? How does the Central Limit Theorem work, and why might a standardized statistic come from a t-distribution instead of a normal distribution? These types of questions are answered without proof in MA223, but formalized in MA382. Students in MA382 use the R programming language for statistical computing and tend to analyze larger datasets. Topics in MA382 are approached from a linear modeling setting as opposed to separate cases in MA223.

    If I have taken MA223 already, may I take MA382 for credit?

    No, students may only take one of these introductory statistics courses. They both cover the same topics, but MA382 gives a deeper understanding of theory and relies more heavily on programming and computing for analyses. If you liked your MA223 class, then you should consider taking an upper-level statistics course such as MA386 in the fall or potentially getting a statistics minor.

    I already took MA381. How does MA382 differ? (Both courses have "probability" and "statistics" in the title.)

    Although the titles may imply that these courses are very similar, the approaches are quite different. Probability is a traditional area of mathematics. Probability assumes that certain population distributions and parameter values are known. Probabilists use that information to perform calculations which may be related to the chance of events occurring. In statistics, the population distributions and parameter values are unknown. You use data in order to estimate parameters and try to learn about the population. In statistics, we spend a lot of time focusing on communication of results and writing. This aspect is also quite different for most students.
